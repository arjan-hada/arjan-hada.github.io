About Me

I am a research scientist passionate about leveraging my expertise in life science and data science to solve meaningful problems. I have executed several cross-functional projects to completion as evidenced by peer-reviewed publications and my projects portfolio page. I’d love to combine my love for learning and deep training to generate insights that can be acted upon.

To reinforce industry data science skills, I have worked on several projects as a part of Data Analyst Nanodegree program from Udacity and currently mentor several students in Udacity courses. During my Ph.D. I have successfully developed novel technologies for taking molecular level snapshots of the basic unit of our 23 pairs of chromosomes to help us better understand the working of our genes in health and disease. I also realized I could be a better scientist at work if I learned computational approaches to problem solving. So I swore off several evenings and weekends working on a specialization in Data Analysis for Life Science from Harvard University and additional bioinformatics and genomics courses.

Specialities:
● Data science tools: Scientific Python: Numpy, Scipy, Pandas, matplotlib, scikit-learn, beautifulsoup4. R and Bioconductor packages: tidyverse, ggvis, leaflet, Shiny.
● Programming Languages: Python, R, and shell scripting.
● Biochemistry and Molecular biology: Biochemical assay development, enzymology, protein purification, protein engineering, nucleic acid analysis and manipulation, chromatography, sequencing gels, crosslinking assays.
● Data visualization: Tableau, ggplot2, matplotlib, Seaborn, plotly, and Bokeh.
● Database: SQL
● Machine learning: Linear & Logistic Regression, Naive Bayes, SVM, Random Forest, Boosting, K-means.
● Communication: Peer coaching, mentoring, writing scientific reports and journal articles, presenting at scientific conferences.
● Design: Markdown, LaTeX, Photoshop, Illustrator, Powerpoint.
● Version Control Systems: Git/Github
